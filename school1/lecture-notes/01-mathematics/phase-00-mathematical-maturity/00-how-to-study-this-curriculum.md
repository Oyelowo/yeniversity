# Lesson 00 - How To Study This Curriculum

## Purpose

The goal is not to read a lot of material. The goal is to become able to derive, solve, explain, and build.

You are studying for engineering depth, which means your standard must be stricter than passive familiarity.

## The Required Loop

For every lesson:

1. Read the definitions carefully.
2. Rewrite the important ideas from memory.
3. Solve exercises without looking at the notes.
4. Mark exactly what you got wrong.
5. Revisit the lesson after a delay.

## What Counts As Understanding

You understand a topic only if you can:

1. State the definitions precisely.
2. Explain why the definitions are shaped that way.
3. Do problems that differ from the examples.
4. Catch a common mistake.
5. Connect the topic to something later in math, physics, computing, or engineering.

## What To Write In Your Notes

Your notes should not be copies of a textbook. They should contain:

1. The essential definitions
2. The main examples
3. The traps and common confusions
4. Your own short summary
5. The questions you could not answer cleanly

## How To Use Exercises

Exercises are not a formality. They are where weak understanding is exposed.

When you miss a problem, do not just write the correct answer. Write:

1. what you thought
2. where the logic broke
3. what rule or definition you ignored

## Progress Rule

Only mark a lesson complete in `PROGRESS.md` when you can do its core tasks without notes.
