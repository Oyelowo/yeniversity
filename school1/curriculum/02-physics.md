# Physics Curriculum

Physics turns mathematics into models of reality. For your goals, physics is not optional; it is the engine behind hardware, vehicles, robotics, sensors, energy systems, and fabrication.

## Stage 1: Mechanics

Topics:

1. Kinematics
2. Newton's laws
3. Work and energy
4. Momentum and collisions
5. Rotational motion
6. Oscillations
7. Rigid body basics

Outputs:

1. Projectile motion Rust simulator
2. Pendulum model
3. Spring-mass-damper comparison between theory and simulation
4. Notes on torque, inertia, and gears for robotics

## Stage 2: Electricity and Magnetism

Topics:

1. Charge, field, and potential
2. Current and resistance
3. Capacitance and inductance
4. Magnetic forces and induction
5. Maxwell-level intuition, then later formalism

Outputs:

1. RC, RL, and RLC simulations
2. Basic field visualization code
3. Notes linking E and M concepts to motors, transformers, sensors, and communication

## Stage 3: Waves, Signals, and Optics

Topics:

1. Wave equation intuition
2. Superposition and interference
3. Resonance and damping
4. Fourier viewpoint introduction
5. Geometric optics
6. Imaging basics

Outputs:

1. Resonance explorer in Rust
2. Basic ray tracing or lens geometry tool
3. Camera and sensor optics notes

## Stage 4: Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer

Topics:

1. Temperature, heat, and work
2. First and second laws
3. Efficiency and irreversibility
4. Conduction, convection, radiation
5. Thermal design intuition for electronics and engines

Outputs:

1. Battery and motor thermal estimate sheets
2. Heat transfer simulation toy model
3. Notes on cooling constraints in real devices

## Stage 5: Modern Physics Exposure

Topics:

1. Atoms and spectra
2. Quantum foundations at intuition level first
3. Solid-state basics later for semiconductors
4. Nuclear basics only as needed

Why it matters:

1. Semiconductor devices
2. Sensors
3. Materials behavior
4. Imaging technologies

## Standard Of Mastery

For every physics topic:

1. Derive the governing equation
2. State the assumptions clearly
3. Predict scaling behavior
4. Simulate the system
5. Connect it to a real mechanism or device
