# Chemistry And Materials Curriculum

Chemistry is not the first focus, but it becomes critical for batteries, semiconductors, fabrication, corrosion, materials choice, sensors, coatings, and energy systems.

## Stage 1: General Chemistry Refresher

Topics:

1. Atomic structure
2. Bonding
3. Stoichiometry
4. Equilibrium
5. Acids and bases
6. Redox

Outputs:

1. Core concept notes
2. Equation balancing and stoichiometry drill sheets
3. Summary sheets linking chemistry to engineering devices

## Stage 2: Thermochemistry And Electrochemistry

Topics:

1. Reaction energetics
2. Gibbs free energy intuition
3. Electrochemical cells
4. Batteries and fuel cells
5. Corrosion and degradation

Outputs:

1. Battery chemistry comparison notes
2. Simple discharge model exercises
3. Materials tradeoff notes for energy systems

## Stage 3: Materials Science Foundations

Topics:

1. Crystal structure basics
2. Defects and grain structure
3. Metals, ceramics, polymers, composites
4. Mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties
5. Failure, fatigue, and corrosion

Outputs:

1. Material selection matrix for projects
2. Notes linking structure to properties
3. Case studies: aircraft, robots, phones, and sensors

## Stage 4: Semiconductor And Fabrication-Relevant Chemistry

Topics:

1. Semiconductor materials basics
2. Doping intuition
3. Oxidation, etching, deposition overview
4. Cleanliness, contamination, and process control

Outputs:

1. Intro semiconductor process notes
2. Fabrication stack map
3. Notes on why modern chip production is hard

## Standard Of Mastery

You are using chemistry correctly when you can explain how microscopic interactions constrain device behavior, manufacturing choices, durability, and performance.
