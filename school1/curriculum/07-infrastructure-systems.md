# Infrastructure And Systems Curriculum

Large infrastructure is engineered coordination at scale. To understand how factories, grids, transport systems, and megaprojects get built, study not only physics but interfaces, sequencing, reliability, and operations.

## Stage 1: Systems Engineering Fundamentals

Topics:

1. Requirements and constraints
2. Architecture and subsystem decomposition
3. Interfaces
4. Verification and validation
5. Reliability and maintainability
6. Risk and failure analysis

Outputs:

1. System architecture note templates
2. Interface definition examples
3. Failure mode and tradeoff sheets

## Stage 2: Structural, Civil, And Energy Awareness

Topics:

1. Loads and safety factors
2. Materials and durability
3. Fluids and pipelines basics
4. Power generation and distribution overview
5. Thermal and environmental constraints

Outputs:

1. Infrastructure reading notes
2. Comparison studies: bridges, rail, factories, data centers, fabs

## Stage 3: Construction And Industrial Delivery

Topics:

1. Scheduling and sequencing
2. Supply chains and procurement
3. Quality assurance
4. Cost estimation basics
5. Operations and maintenance thinking

Outputs:

1. Case study notes on how large systems are delivered
2. Build sequencing breakdowns for real-world examples

## Stage 4: Manufacturing Systems

Topics:

1. Production lines
2. Tooling and fixtures
3. Yield, scrap, and throughput
4. Automation and control
5. Metrology and process discipline

Outputs:

1. Notes on phone, car, aircraft, and chip manufacturing stacks
2. Fabrication toolchain maps
