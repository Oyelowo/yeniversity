# Control And Robotics Curriculum

This is where mechanical systems, sensors, computation, and feedback become machines with behavior.

## Stage 1: Signals And Systems

Topics:

1. Continuous and discrete signals
2. Convolution intuition
3. Frequency response
4. Laplace and z-transform exposure
5. Sampling and aliasing

Outputs:

1. Signal generation and filtering tools in Rust
2. Notes connecting time-domain and frequency-domain views

## Stage 2: Control Theory

Topics:

1. Feedback concepts
2. Transfer functions
3. Step response and stability
4. PID control
5. State-space basics
6. Controllability and observability

Outputs:

1. PID simulator
2. DC motor model and controller
3. Stability notes from worked examples

## Stage 3: Estimation And Sensor Fusion

Topics:

1. Noise models
2. Filtering intuition
3. Kalman filter basics
4. IMU, encoder, and GPS fusion ideas

Outputs:

1. Rust estimator prototypes
2. Logged synthetic dataset experiments
3. Drift and uncertainty notes

## Stage 4: Robotics

Topics:

1. Kinematics
2. Dynamics
3. Motion planning
4. Localization basics
5. Perception integration

Outputs:

1. Two-link arm simulator
2. Differential drive robot model
3. Capstone robotics project plan

## Stage 5: Domain Extensions

Possible branches:

1. Drones and aerial robotics
2. Ground vehicles and autonomy
3. Manipulators and industrial robotics
4. Camera and vision-heavy robotic systems
