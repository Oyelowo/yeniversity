# Exercise Sheet 01 - Propositions, Truth Values, And Connectives

Complete these without looking back at the lesson if possible.

## Part A - Identify Propositions

For each item, decide whether it is a proposition. If it is, state whether it is true or false.

1. `5 is an odd number.`
2. `Please open the window.`
3. `x^2 = 9`
4. `The Pacific Ocean is larger than the Atlantic Ocean.`
5. `What time is it?`
6. `0 is greater than 1.`

## Part B - Negation

Let `p` be the proposition `The motor is running.` Write the meaning of:

1. `!p`

Let `q` be the proposition `The battery voltage is above 12V.` Write the meaning of:

2. `!q`

## Part C - Conjunction And Disjunction

Let:

- `p`: `The drone has GPS lock.`
- `q`: `The drone has enough battery to return home.`

Write in plain English:

1. `p ∧ q`
2. `p ∨ q`
3. `!p ∧ q`
4. `p ∨ !q`

## Part D - Truth Tables

Construct full truth tables for:

1. `!p`
2. `p ∧ q`
3. `p ∨ q`
4. `!(p ∧ q)`

## Part E - Evaluate Compound Statements

Let:

- `p`: `2 < 5`
- `q`: `10 is prime`

Find the truth value of:

1. `p ∧ q`
2. `p ∨ q`
3. `!q`
4. `!p ∨ q`

## Part F - Reflection

Answer briefly:

1. Why is `x + 1 = 4` not automatically a proposition?
2. Why does inclusive `or` matter in mathematics and engineering logic?
3. What mistake do you think you are most likely to make in this lesson?
